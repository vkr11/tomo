# Developing the Saga Method for Interviews — Detailed Log

## Context

The user was looking for a better way to structure behavioral interview stories, finding the standard STAR method insufficient for the complexity of senior leadership roles.

## Timeline

### Phase 1: Evaluation & Hierarchy Definition

**Goal:** Understand the existing structure and propose a better hierarchy.

- Evaluated existing templates (`mission`, `epic`, `story`) → Found they were solid but needed a clear overarching narrative.
- Proposed the "Saga → Epic → Story" hierarchy — _Reasoning: Emphasizes career chapters and narrative arcs over HR job titles._
- Renamed "Role" to "Saga" per user preference.

### Phase 2: Refinement & Principles as Lenses

**Goal:** Turn the hierarchy into a functional interview system.

- Introduced "Principles as Lenses" — _Reasoning: Allows the same story to be retold through different competency filters (e.g., Conflict vs. Failure)._
- Developed the "Supply vs. Demand" framing — _Reasoning: Separates the user's fixed history from the interviewer's changing rubric._
- Added "The Read" concept for real-time room assessment.

### Phase 3: Genericization & Finalization

**Goal:** Make the documentation shareable and commit to git.

- Rewrote `approach.md` as `the_saga_method.md`.
- Removed specific Meta/Privacy examples → Replaced with E-commerce and Health-Tech scenarios.
- Committed and pushed to ` origin/main` in the `tomo` repository.

## Decisions & Rationale

| Decision | Alternatives Considered | Why This Choice |
|---|---|---|
| Using "Saga" as top layer | Role, Chapter, Mission | Saga implies a grand narrative arc and epic scale suitable for senior roles. |
| Making Principle a Lens | Principle as a Tag | A tag is passive; a lens is an active delivery choice that reshapes the story narrative. |
| Supply vs Demand framing | Integrated model | Makes it clear that the candidate's material is portable across any company rubric. |
