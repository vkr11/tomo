# 50 Lessons From 50 Years Lived

*Takeaways from my half-century journey*

[![](images/50-lessons-from-50-years-lived/56be2e3defce.jpeg)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!jI-7!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fdfd52cdd-d496-4772-9082-d6581086fde2_1800x1200.jpeg)

I used to hate birthdays when I was a kid. No matter what I was doing or how old I was, it always felt like it was such a disappointment. My parents were immigrants without a lot of money. I grew up in a town where I never felt accepted, and birthdays were just a reminder of those things.

I remember one year when my mom was away from home for my birthday, so I baked my own cake. My dad took me shooting with his BB guns in the backyard. It was pretty depressing, actually, and I remember thinking, “Birthdays suck.” I was 9 or 10.

As I grew older, birthdays seemed like something to be tolerated, just another day in the year. But I wanted my children to have a very different experience with birthdays. I wanted to make each birthday special in some way. Except a funny thing happened. They also treat birthdays super casually. I ask them every year if they want to have a party, and almost every time, they say no. Instead we just go out to eat and have a small celebration.

A few years ago, my friend’s husband died. Prior to his passing, she dreaded birthdays, but now sees every single birthday as something to be cherished. So on this, my 50th birthday, I want to commemorate the occasion by sharing what I have learned over the years.

I have been writing this blog for over five years and in that time have posted over 250 articles. Many of these lessons I learned the hard way. Looking across everything, these are the 50 lessons that mattered most.

### **Identity and Direction**

#### 1. *Define success before the world defines it for you.*

If you do not articulate your own mission, you will optimize for someone else’s metrics. Writing a personal mission statement forces clarity and alignment.

#### 2. *The company you keep quietly shapes who you are.*

Who you surround yourself with can influence your habits, mindset, and decisions, in more ways than you realize. Choose your circle carefully.

#### 3. *Treat life like a beta test; each day is a prototype.*

We fear letting go or changing course, but sometimes pivots aren’t detours; they *are* the path.

#### 4. *Scarcity buries possibilities. An abundance mindset creates them.*

Scarcity teaches us to view the world through a zero-sum lens, obscuring solutions and shrinking our world. But an abundance mindset allows you to discover win-wins you never considered.

#### 5. *Choose the story you tell about yourself.*

If we do not take initiative in the narrative of our lives, it takes control of us. Change the story, change your life.

### **Career Foundations**

#### 6. *Product manage your career like you PM your product.*

Product managers build roadmaps. Few people build them for their own careers. Treat your life with the same consideration.

#### 7. *Learning to work with anyone is a secret superpower that unlocks new opportunities.*

You will not always be able to choose who you work with. Inevitably, you are going clash with someone, or someone is going to clash with you. Knowing how to diffuse conflict and find common ground is a crucial skill.

#### 8. *Making friends at work can yield unlimited dividends.*

Conventional wisdom says to clock in and out without getting too attached. But your colleagues can be powerful allies not just at work, but in life.

#### 9. *Build your career beyond your job and keep your eye on your broader learning.*

As your competency increases in a role, your execution gets better, but your learning suffers. Your current role is just one chapter in your overall journey. Map accordingly.

#### 10. *Networking is not a dirty word but rather an opportunity for connection.*

Change the way you look at networking, from transactional to connection.

#### 11. *When you enter a new role, preparation is key to landing well.*

There are many pitfalls and challenges when it comes to starting a new job, whether it be a move within your existing org or a new one. Set yourself up for success.

#### 12. *Change is inevitable, but we can choose what comes next.*

Many things in life happen that we don’t prefer, and even when the change is something long-awaited, navigating it can still be challenging. There is no roadmap for handling life’s transitions, but the good part is that we get to choose how we respond.

#### 13. *Having children may alter your career timeline, but you can stay true to the trajectory.*

Maternity leave taught me that roles change who we are, not just what we do.

#### 14. *If you aren’t thriving, maybe it’s time to plant yourself in new soil.*

Find a place where you are in the right conditions to thrive, and see who you can become without the constraints of where you have been.

#### 15. *There are no shortcuts. Sometimes the only way out is through.*

Avoidance doesn’t make the change go away. The next steps may not be easy, but neither is staying where you are.

### **Leadership**

#### 16. *Management is a learned craft, not just a stepping stone up the ladder.*

Being good at your job doesn’t mean you’ll automatically be a great manager. Management is its own skill, and if you want to manage people, you need to learn it.

#### 17. *Learn how to solicit feedback to grow faster and gain more than you imagined.*

No leader is perfect. At every level of your career, there will be room for improvement, yet at higher-level positions, it is harder to come by. Learn to ask for feedback so you can keep growing.

#### 18. *Think like a founder (even if you aren’t one) to unlock both skills and opportunities.*

Great founders have a lot to teach us about leadership, courage, and creativity. The mindset of a founder takes your work from ordinary to remarkable.

#### 19. *Extreme clarity is the ultimate gift for yourself and your team.*

Better a clear “no” than a messy “yes”. Misalignment stalls progress and creates rifts among teams. Drive extreme clarity to give everyone the best path forward.

#### 20. *Regardless of your title, executive presence is one of the most important skills in organizations.*

Knowing how to appeal to those higher up on the ladder is a useful skill for any career stage.

### **Influence and Communication**

#### 21. *Connection, not words, is the key to influence.*

It’s not about being right, it’s about finding common ground.

#### 22. *How you say it is just as important as what you say.*

How you speak shapes what people think of you. There is power in using your voice.

#### 23. *Give the gift of feedback by showing, not telling.*

Giving and receiving feedback is necessary, but often difficult. Delivery determines reception. Learn how to give feedback in a way that feels like a gift, not an attack.

#### 24. *Being impressive and making an impression are two different things.*

The greatest leaders aren’t necessarily the smartest or loudest people in the room. They’re the ones who make others feel valued and understood.

---

### **Systems and Incentives**

#### 25. *Execution fails because of systems, not effort. Find ways to change the system, not just the individuals.*

It’s the little things that slow down productivity and create interpersonal strife. Instead of blaming people, examine the processes and systems in place.

#### 26. *Misaligned incentives distort entire systems, so find incentives that align teams.*

From academia to tech, reward structures drive unintended outcomes.

#### 27. *Small efficiencies compound into big points of leverage.*

Ten hours saved monthly becomes weeks annually. Focus on leverage to save time and multiply your impact.

#### 28. *Product strategy begins with empathy.*

Vision without customer pain is theater.

---

### **Perspective**

#### 29. *Data can change the way you look at the world. Leverage charts to change your perspective.*

The charts that linger often challenge assumptions and make visible what was once invisible.

#### 30. *Solve for joy, and the rest will come.*

Ambition without joy becomes brittle. Find what fills you up and build around it.

#### 31. *Regret holds you down. Live life moving forward, not looking back.*

Regret is the act of looking backward. The more steps you take while looking back, the fewer steps you take while looking forward to where you could go.

#### 32. *Forward motion requires getting unstuck from something holding you back.*

Clarity often follows action.

---

### **Mentors, Sponsors, and Coaches**

#### 33. *A mentor advises. A sponsor opens doors. Find a sponsor to make a step function in your career.*

Both are integral for not only advancing your career, but finding meaning and purpose along the way.

#### 34. *Credit is infinitely divisible. Generosity is a gift that pays forward.*

Another mentor taught me to give more credit than necessary. Influence grows when ego shrinks.

#### *35. Grow your coaching tree; pay the wisdom forward.*

When someone pours into you, find people you can do the same for. You’ll be surprised by how much of an impact you can make in the lives of others.

### Life and Integration

#### 36. *Technology amplifies leverage, and AI will reward those who learn to harness the technology.*

AI will not replace judgment; it will reward it.

#### 37. *Becoming a connector multiplies your impact.*

Learn to see each relationship as a potential opportunity for creating a connection between two people who otherwise may not have met.

#### 38. *Rituals carry legacy. Honor the traditions that made you who you are.*

Food, traditions, shared moments outlast titles. Create a legacy though understanding your family journey.

#### 39. *Openness accelerates growth. Give yourself the space to expand the aperture and see greater possibilities.*

A Year of Yes broadens your horizons and carves paths you never saw before. Learn how to make space to explore.

#### 40. *Writing clarifies thinking. Create, don’t consume, to open your mind.*

Consistency in writing sharpened my voice and convictions.

---

### **Enduring Truths**

#### 41. *Ask what someone is optimizing for, and you learn what makes them tick.*

Conflict often hides incentive misalignment. Alignment happens through mutual understanding.

#### 42. *Recognition and gratitude means more to others than you think.*

Lift others up through praise and create more opportunity for connection.

#### 43. *Leading through others means enabling them to do their best work.*

The higher you rise, the more your job is not to do but to lead through others and their success.

#### 44. *Adding instead of subtracting means you know what truly matters in your life.*

The sunk cost fallacy and loss aversion can hold you back. Release your anchors and learn to let go.

#### 45. *Hard conversations and feedback are accelerants.*

Avoidance prolongs dysfunction. Ask the hard questions to get to the truth.

#### 46. *Success may look different from what you were taught.*

Many “rules” about career progression were built for a different era. Interrogate assumptions before you obey them.

#### 47. *Get rid of the maybes in your life. Let your yes be yes and no be no.*

Prioritizing ruthlessly creates space for what actually matters. A fast no is better than a slow yes.

#### 48. *Don’t mistake motion for progress.*

Busyness can be a disguise for stagnation. Separate the signal from the noise.

#### 49. *The long road is still the road that you can traverse with enough time and planning.*

We often think our aspirations are out of reach, but with a clear plan and some discipline, nearly anything is possible.

#### 50. *You are still becoming, no matter what the age.*

Every version of you was preparation for who you are today. The next version of you is being created every day.
