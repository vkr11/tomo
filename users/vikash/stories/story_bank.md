### 1. Responsible & Private AI / Ads Fairness Compliance

- **Mission**: Responsible & Private AI / Ads Fairness Compliance (Meta, 2021–2023)
- **One-liner**: Turn iOS14 plus regulatory pressure (HUD/DoJ, EU DMA) into durable privacy and fairness infrastructure across PETs/IPA, VRS, LOV, and reporting.
- **Intro pitch**:
  - **Context**: iOS14, cookie deprecation, and HUD/DoJ fairness requirements put Meta's core ads measurement and delivery systems under real pressure.
  - **Challenge**: Reinvent measurement, targeting, and compliance under privacy and regulatory constraints without breaking advertiser value.
  - **What I Did**: Defined the product strategy across PETs/IPA, VRS, LOV, and compliance infrastructure — identified IPA as the highest-leverage industry bet, drove Meta's position at W3C PATCG, and aligned Ads, Policy, and Legal leadership on a multi-workstream roadmap.
  - **Impact**: Built durable privacy and fairness infrastructure that met regulatory needs, protected business value, and created a long-term competitive moat.
- **Why this matters**: This is the clearest example of turning external platform and policy shocks into product strategy, technical systems, and defensible infrastructure — with cross-industry influence.
- **Signature proof points**:
  - Identified IPA as Meta's strategic bet for privacy-preserving measurement, co-authored the proposal with Mozilla, and represented Meta's position at W3C PATCG.
  - Shaped the VRS approach using BISG and Differential Privacy; met the DOJ mandate of under 10% variance for 91.7% of housing ads.
  - Defined the AFS strategy toward less personalized ads to help preserve a EUR107B EU ad market under DMA pressure.
- **Best epics to zoom into**:
  - PETs / IPA
  - VRS (Variance Reduction System)
  - HUD / DoJ Compliance
  - AFS (Ads Free Subscription)

### 4. Real-World Identity & Growth Graph

- **Mission**: Real-World Identity & Growth Graph (Meta, 2019–2021)
- **One-liner**: Grow and secure the real-world friend graph and identity system across relationship inference and device/people graph work while defending against abuse and enumeration.
- **Intro pitch**:
  - **Context**: The real-world friend graph and identity system sat underneath some of Meta's most important growth and integrity experiences.
  - **Challenge**: Preserve the growth value of identity systems for onboarding, connection, and relevance while defending against abuse, enumeration, and privacy risk.
  - **What I Did**: Owned the product strategy for relationship inference and the device/people graph — set the direction for balancing growth investment against adversarial defense, and after the 533M-record contact importer breach, led the strategic shift toward hardened abuse resistance.
  - **Impact**: Delivered identity capabilities that drove 1%+ MAU impact on Facebook and Instagram while making the graph materially more resilient to attack.
- **Why this matters**: This mission is the strongest example of balancing growth and integrity in a system that is both strategically valuable and constantly under attack — including navigating a major crisis moment.
- **Signature proof points**:
  - Real World Friend Graph drove over 1% MAU impact for Facebook and Instagram.
  - The graph powered ~5% of all Messenger conversations.
  - After the 533M-record contact importer abuse incident, defined and led the strategic pivot toward adversarial defenses including rate limiting, behavioral analysis, and data limiting.
- **Best epics to zoom into**:
  - Real World Friend Graph
  - People to Device Graph
  - New Device Experiences
