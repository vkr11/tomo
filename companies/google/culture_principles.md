# Google — Culture & Principles

> Google’s culture is driven by its "Ten things we know to be true" and a heavy emphasis on "Googleyness" — a blend of intellectual humility, comfort with ambiguity, and a relentless focus on the user.

---

## 1. Focus on the user and all else will follow
*Since the beginning, we’ve focused on providing the best user experience possible. Whether we’re designing a new Internet browser or a new tweak to the look of the homepage, we take great care to ensure that they will ultimately serve you, rather than our own internal goal or bottom line.*

**Interview Signal**: Show that you prioritize user needs and pain points over technical feasibility or business constraints in the initial stages of product design.

---

## 2. Fast is better than slow
*We know your time is valuable, so when you’re seeking an answer on the web you want it right away–and we aim to please.*

**Interview Signal**: Demonstrate an ability to prioritize ruthlessly, ship MVPs quickly to gather data, and iterate based on fast feedback cycles.

---

## 3. Democracy on the web works
*Google search works because it relies on the millions of individuals posting links on websites to help determine which other sites offer content of value.*

**Interview Signal**: Show that you leverage data, consensus-building, and cross-functional input to make decisions, rather than relying solely on top-down authority.

---

## 4. Great just isn't good enough
*We see being great at something as a starting point, not an endpoint. We set ourselves goals we know we can’t reach yet, because we know that by stretching to meet them we can get further than we expected.*

**Interview Signal**: Emphasize "10x thinking" (moonshots) over incremental 10% improvements when brainstorming solutions in product design rounds.

---

## 5. Googleyness (Core Trait)
*A candidate's cultural fit, demonstrating qualities such as honesty, kindness, comfort with ambiguity, a strong sense of ownership, and humility. It assesses how individuals work independently and as part of a team.*

**Interview Signal**: Admit when you don't know something, share credit with your team, and show that you can thrive when given a vague, unstructured problem without clear direction.

---

## Principle Priority Tiers

| Priority Tier | Principles | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — Always tested** | Focus on the user, Googleyness | These are the absolute core of Google's PM evaluation. Every product design question tests user focus; every behavioral tests Googleyness. |
| **Tier 2 — Frequently tested** | Great just isn't good enough (10x thinking), Fast is better than slow | Evaluates your ability to scale solutions globally and navigate Google's complex, data-heavy execution environment. |
| **Tier 3 — Differentiators** | Democracy on the web works | Shows you can lead by influence rather than authority (critical in Google's engineering-led culture). |

> **Pro Tip**: In any product sense question, always start by defining the user segments and their specific pain points before jumping into solutions. Google interviewers will specifically dock points if you skip the user empathy phase.
