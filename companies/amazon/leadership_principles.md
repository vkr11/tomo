# Amazon's 16 Leadership Principles

> Amazon's LPs are the **single most important** interview dimension. Every behavioral question maps to one or more LPs. The Bar Raiser will specifically evaluate your LP fluency.

---

## 1. Customer Obsession
*Leaders start with the customer and work backward. They work vigorously to earn and keep customer trust. Although leaders pay attention to competitors, they obsess over customers.*

**Interview Signal**: Show you made a decision that prioritized the customer *over* internal convenience or short-term metrics.

## 2. Ownership
*Leaders are owners. They think long term and don't sacrifice long-term value for short-term results. They act on behalf of the entire company, beyond just their own team. They never say "that's not my job."*

**Interview Signal**: Show you took accountability for something *outside* your defined scope because it was the right thing to do.

## 3. Invent and Simplify
*Leaders expect and require innovation and invention from their teams and always find ways to simplify. They are externally aware, look for new ideas from everywhere, and are not limited by "not invented here."*

**Interview Signal**: Show a creative solution that *reduced* complexity, not just added a feature.

## 4. Are Right, A Lot
*Leaders are right a lot. They have strong judgment and good instincts. They seek diverse perspectives and work to disconfirm their beliefs.*

**Interview Signal**: Show a decision where you actively sought out disagreement, changed your mind, or bet against conventional wisdom — and were proven right.

## 5. Learn and Be Curious
*Leaders are never done learning and always seek to improve themselves. They are curious about new possibilities and act to explore them.*

**Interview Signal**: Show a concrete domain pivot or upskilling moment that directly improved your work output.

## 6. Hire and Develop the Best
*Leaders raise the performance bar with every hire and promotion. They recognize exceptional talent, and willingly move them throughout the organization. Leaders develop leaders and take seriously their role in coaching others.*

**Interview Signal**: Talk about a specific hire you advocated for, or how you developed someone beyond expectations.

## 7. Insist on the Highest Standards
*Leaders have relentlessly high standards — many people may think these standards are unreasonably high. Leaders are continually raising the bar and driving their teams to deliver high-quality products, services, and processes. Leaders ensure that defects do not get sent down the line and that problems are fixed so they stay fixed.*

**Interview Signal**: Show a time you pushed back on "good enough" and quantify the quality delta.

## 8. Think Big
*Thinking small is a self-fulfilling prophecy. Leaders create and communicate a bold direction that inspires results. They think differently and look around corners for ways to serve customers.*

**Interview Signal**: Present a vision you set that was 10x the original scope, and show how you inspired others to pursue it.

## 9. Bias for Action
*Speed matters in business. Many decisions and actions are reversible and do not need extensive study. We value calculated risk taking.*

**Interview Signal**: Show a decision you made with imperfect data under time pressure, and explain why waiting would have been costlier.

## 10. Frugality
*Accomplish more with less. Constraints breed resourcefulness, self-sufficiency, and invention. There are no extra points for growing headcount, budget size, or fixed expense.*

**Interview Signal**: Show a result you achieved with *fewer* resources than expected, not by doing less but by being creative.

## 11. Earn Trust
*Leaders listen attentively, speak candidly, and treat others respectfully. They are vocally self-critical, even when doing so is awkward or embarrassing. Leaders do not believe their or their team's body odor smells of perfume. They benchmark themselves and their teams against the best.*

**Interview Signal**: Show vulnerability — admitting a mistake to a stakeholder, being self-critical, or earning trust from a skeptic.

## 12. Dive Deep
*Leaders operate at all levels, stay connected to the details, audit frequently, and are skeptical when metrics and anecdote differ. No task is beneath them.*

**Interview Signal**: Show a time you personally dug into the data/code/logs and found something everyone else missed.

## 13. Have Backbone; Disagree and Commit
*Leaders are obligated to respectfully challenge decisions when they disagree, even when doing so is uncomfortable or exhausting. Leaders have conviction and are tenacious. They do not compromise for the sake of social cohesion. Once a decision is determined, they commit wholly.*

**Interview Signal**: Show a disagreement where you pushed back *and* a case where you committed after losing the argument.

## 14. Deliver Results
*Leaders focus on the key inputs for their business and deliver them with the right quality and in a timely fashion. Despite setbacks, they rise to the occasion and never settle.*

**Interview Signal**: Lead with the metric. "$X revenue", "Y% improvement", "Z users served." Make it impossible to miss.

## 15. Strive to be Earth's Best Employer
*Leaders work every day to create a safer, more productive, higher performing, more diverse, and more just work environment. They lead with empathy, have fun at work, and make it easy for others to have fun.*

**Interview Signal**: Show how you invested in team culture, psychological safety, or inclusion beyond your job description.

## 16. Success and Scale Bring Broad Responsibility
*We started in a garage, but we're not there anymore. We are big, we impact the world, and we are far from perfect. We must be humble and thoughtful about even the secondary effects of our actions.*

**Interview Signal**: Show awareness of the societal impact of your product decisions and what safeguards you put in place.

---

## LP Interview Cheat Sheet

| Priority Tier | LPs | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Tier 1 — Always tested** | Customer Obsession, Ownership, Deliver Results | Core to every PM role |
| **Tier 2 — Bar Raiser favorites** | Are Right A Lot, Dive Deep, Disagree & Commit, Earn Trust | These are the "depth" probes |
| **Tier 3 — Differentiators** | Think Big, Invent & Simplify, Bias for Action | These separate "good" from "great" |
| **Tier 4 — Senior-level** | Hire & Develop, Highest Standards, Frugality | Expected at L6+/IC7+ |
| **Tier 5 — Newer principles** | Earth's Best Employer, Broad Responsibility | Less tested but good to reference |

> **Pro Tip**: Prepare 2 stories per Tier 1-2 LP, 1 per Tier 3-4. Always use **"I"** not "we" — Amazon cares about *your* specific contribution.
