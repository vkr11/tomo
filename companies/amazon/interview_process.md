# Amazon PM Interview Process

> **Role Target**: Senior PM (L6) / Principal PM (L7)

---

## Interview Stages

| Stage | Duration | Format | Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Recruiter Screen** | 60 min | Phone | Background, motivation, LP surface-level |
| **2. PM Phone Interview** | 60 min | Phone/Video | Product sense, strategy, customer obsession |
| **3. Written Assessment** | 1-2 pages | Async (2-day turnaround) | Structured thinking, clarity, customer focus |
| **4. Interview Loop** | 4-6 rounds × 45-60 min | Onsite/Virtual | Full behavioral + product assessment |

---

## The Loop — Round Breakdown (Typical)

| Round | Interviewer | Focus | Key LPs |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Hiring Manager | Product depth, team fit | Customer Obsession, Ownership |
| 2 | Team PM/Peer | Execution, metrics | Deliver Results, Dive Deep |
| 3 | XFN Partner (Eng/Design) | Collaboration, technical acumen | Earn Trust, Invent & Simplify |
| 4 | **Bar Raiser** ⚠️ | Overall LP assessment, long-term potential | Are Right A Lot, Highest Standards |
| 5 | Senior Leader | Vision, strategy, think big | Think Big, Have Backbone |

---

## The Bar Raiser — What You Need to Know

- **Who**: Senior Amazonian (3+ years) from a *different* team — they're deliberately unbiased
- **Power**: Can **veto** a hire even if every other interviewer says yes
- **Tests for**: Long-term Amazon potential, not just role fit
- **Style**: Deep follow-up questions ("tell me more", "what specifically did YOU do", "what would you do differently")
- **Debrief role**: Chairs the post-loop discussion; focuses on factual data points, not gut feel

### How to Win the Bar Raiser Round
1. **Use "I" relentlessly** — they will probe any "we" statement
2. **Quantify everything** — "improved retention" ≠ "improved D7 retention by 18%"
3. **Show self-awareness** — proactively share what you'd do differently ("In hindsight...")
4. **Demonstrate LP depth** — one strong LP signal per answer isn't enough; layer 2-3 LPs naturally

## Written Assessment Tips

The essay is typically a **Working Backwards** prompt:
- "Imagine you're launching X for Amazon customers..."  
- "Write a 1-page press release for a new feature that solves Y..."

**Structure to follow**:
1. **Customer Problem** (2-3 sentences, specific and empathetic)
2. **Proposed Solution** (crisp, bold, simple)
3. **How It Works** (3-5 bullet points)
4. **Key Metrics** (how you'd measure success)
5. **Why Now** (urgency, market timing)

---

## Amazon-Specific Communication Rules

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Start with the result/conclusion (BLUF) | Build up to a reveal |
| Say "I decided... I built... I shipped..." | Say "we", "the team", "my org" |
| Cite specific metrics and dates | Use vague language ("significantly improved") |
| Reference an LP by name naturally | Force LP names awkwardly into every sentence |
| Acknowledge failures and learnings | Pretend everything was perfect |
