# Research Sources

A curated list of high-quality sources for company interview prep research. Use these to guide your searches in Step 1 (Discover) of the research protocol.

| Company | URL Pattern | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Amazon | aboutamazon.com/about-us/leadership-principles | All 16 LPs with definitions |
| Google | careers.google.com, about.google | Look for "How We Hire" section |
| Meta | about.meta.com/company-info | Meta's cultural values |
| Apple | apple.com/careers | Minimal public culture info — infer from job postings |

## Community Intel

| Source | How to Search | Signal Quality |
|---|---|---|
| **Glassdoor** | `site:glassdoor.com {company} product manager interview` | Medium — volume helps, but noisy |
| **Blind** | `site:teamblind.com {company} PM interview` | High — verified employees |
| **Reddit** | `site:reddit.com r/ProductManagement {company}` | Medium — look for detailed writeups |
| **Levels.fyi** | levels.fyi/companies/{company} | High for comp data, low for interview intel |

## Strategic Analysis Sources

These help understand the company's current strategy, which is critical for "Why this company?" and product sense questions.

| Source | Best For |
|---|---|
| **Stratechery** (stratechery.com) | Deep strategic analysis of tech companies |
| **The Verge** | Product launches, industry news |
| **TechCrunch** | Funding, org changes, new products |
| **Company earnings calls** | Official strategic priorities (search: `{company} earnings call transcript Q{N} {year}`) |
| **Company engineering blogs** | Technical culture signals |

## Search Tips

1. **Add year to searches** — always append `2025 2026` to get recent content
2. **Use site: for community sources** — `site:glassdoor.com` is more targeted than searching Glassdoor via Google
3. **Check multiple prep sites** — Exponent and IGotAnOffer often have different insights on the same company
4. **Look for "Day in the Life" content** — blog posts or YouTube videos from current/former PMs reveal cultural nuances that official pages don't
5. **Earnings calls are underrated** — they reveal what the company actually prioritizes vs. what their careers page says
