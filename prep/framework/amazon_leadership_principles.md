# Amazon Leadership Principles & Career Mapping

> **Purpose**: This guide maps the 16 Amazon Leadership Principles to your specific career Stories/Epics. Use this as a lookup table during interview prep to ensure every answer demonstrates a specific leadership behavior.

---

## 1. Customer Obsession
*Leaders start with the customer and work backward. They work vigorously to earn and keep customer trust.*

*   **Your Map**:
    *   **Epic**: WhatsApp Marketing Messages (MM)
    *   **Story**: Launching "Less Personalized Ads" (LPA) in EU.
    *   **Angle**: You prioritized user choice/privacy (EU regulation) while maintaining a free service, rather than forcing a "pay-only" model that would churn users.
    *   **Epic**: Llama Safety
    *   **Story**: "Purple Teaming" for Child Safety.
    *   **Angle**: Obsessing over protecting the *end-users* (children) from harm, even when developers just wanted raw model performance.

## 2. Ownership
*Leaders act on behalf of the entire company. They never say "that's not my job."*

*   **Your Map**:
    *   **Epic**: Real World Friend Graph
    *   **Story**: "Ask for help sooner" / Managing 38-person team.
    *   **Angle**: You stepped up to manage an eng/XFN team of 38 when PM headcount was missing. You owned the delivery AND the gap.
    *   **Epic**: Scraping Defense
    *   **Story**: Shifting from "TOS Issue" to "Cyber Defense."
    *   **Angle**: You took ownership of a "platform leak" that wasn't technically your bug, but was hurting the company brand.

## 3. Invent and Simplify
*Leaders expect and require innovation and invention from their teams and always find ways to simplify.*

*   **Your Map**:
    *   **Epic**: VRS (Variance Reduction System)
    *   **Story**: Building BISG for demographic inference.
    *   **Angle**: You invented a way to measure race/gender *without* collecting it (SIMPLIFYING privacy compliance) to solve a complex DOJ mandate.
    *   **Epic**: PETs / IPA
    *   **Story**: "Match Keys" & MPC.
    *   **Angle**: Simplified the complex mess of post-cookie attribution into a clean, cryptographic protocol.

## 4. Are Right, A Lot
*Leaders have strong judgment and good instincts. They seek diverse perspectives and work to disconfirm their beliefs.*

*   **Your Map**:
    *   **Epic**: WhatsApp MM Revenue
    *   **Story**: Betting on "Click-to-WhatsApp" (CTWA) vs. just Cold Outbound.
    *   **Angle**: You judged that *inbound* user intent (ads) was a safer, higher-ROI bet than spammy outbound messaging, leading to $10B revenue.
    *   **Epic**: Llama Safety
    *   **Story**: "Open Weights" Safety Strategy.
    *   **Angle**: You bet that transparency (CyberSec Eval) would build more trust than "black box" safety, countering the industry trend.

## 5. Learn and Be Curious
*Leaders are never done learning and always seek to improve themselves.*

*   **Your Map**:
    *   **Epic**: MSL (Meta Superintelligence Labs)
    *   **Story**: Transitioning to GenAI / LLMs.
    *   **Angle**: You pivoted from Ads/Privacy (structured data) to GenAI (unstructured probabilistic models) and mastered the new stack (RLHF, CoT).
    *   **Epic**: Pre-Meta (Infinera)
    *   **Story**: Moving from Hardware (ASIC) to Software (SDN).

## 6. Hire and Develop the Best
*Leaders raise the performance bar with every hire and promotion.*

*   **Your Map**:
    *   **Epic**: Growth / Friend Graph
    *   **Story**: Stabilizing the team.
    *   **Angle**: You identified the need for 2 specific PM profiles, hired them to backfill your own gaps, and mentored them to success.
    *   **Epic**: MSL
    *   **Story**: "GenAI Bootcamp for PMs."
    *   **Angle**: You aren't just hiring; you are *developing* the entire PM org (800+ people) to be AI-native.

## 7. Insist on the Highest Standards
*Leaders have relentlessly high standards. They empower their people to deliver high-quality products, services, and processes.*

*   **Your Map**:
    *   **Epic**: HEC Ads / VRS
    *   **Story**: Hitting the 91.7% / 10% variance target.
    *   **Angle**: The DOJ mandate was grueling. You insisted on accurate measurement (BISG) rather than "good enough" proxies, ensuring legal bulletproofing.
    *   **Epic**: Llama Safety
    *   **Story**: Lowering "False Refusal" rates.
    *   **Angle**: You didn't settle for a safe model that was useless; you tuned Llama Guard to be precise.

## 8. Think Big

## How to Use This Matrix

1.  **Identify the Question**: Is the interviewer asking about "Conflict"? "Innovation"? "Failure"?
2.  **Select the Principle**: "Conflict" -> *Have Backbone*. "Innovation" -> *Invent and Simplify*.
3.  **Select the Story**: Look up the mapped story (e.g., AFS Pivot or VRS BISG).
4.  **STAR It**: Deliver the story using Situation-Task-Action-Result.
